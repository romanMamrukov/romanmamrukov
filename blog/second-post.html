Personal website from idea to a million iterations

The story of my personal website goes all the way back to 2014. Back then, I was studying a Games Development course and didn’t want to be the only one among my fellow programmer classmates without a personal page. The first version was simple — just enough to have an online presence and a place to link to my projects.

As time went on, my goals expanded. I wanted the site not only to showcase my work but also to help me attract clients for website development and possibly land job opportunities. Around 2015–2016, I decided to experiment with self-hosting on a small nettop computer using Apache and PHP. For a while, it was a fun setup — running from my own home — until I had to move, and the website project was set aside for a few years.

In 2020, I revived the site with a simpler, more modern look, this time focusing on making it clean, easy to navigate, and a better representation of my skills. Then, starting in 2024 and continuing into 2025, I began iterating more frequently, adding new features, improving performance, and experimenting with the design. Each update was a learning opportunity — from responsive layouts to better SEO structure and accessibility tweaks.

Today, the site stands in what I’d call its “semi-final” form. I’m happy with how it looks and functions, though I know there will always be small improvements to make. That’s the beauty of a personal website: it’s never truly finished. It grows and evolves alongside my own skills, ideas, and ambitions.
